Research

My Research work in collaboration with Prof.Konda Reddy was on Adversarial Robustness of Deep Neural Networks.
Current SOTA on Robustness relies on Adversarial Training along with additional Parametrization and Regularization, which causes a huge increase in time to train compared to standard training. Our aim was to make a DNN robust without the additional time overbound of Adversarial Training. For this, we performed feature space perturbation, training models on a convex combination of different images on a particular feature space or set of feature space. This happens on the fly, hence takes the same time as normal training. Using this method, we achieved an accuracy of 32.37% on VGG19 with a PGD (200 steps, CIFAR10-Linf) attack, which is close to the accuracy obtained by Adversarial Training. However, the approach did not generalize to ResNet and DenseNet, with the most likely explanation of Residual additionals altering the convex combination in later layers.

On a hindsight, I strongly feel Adversarial Robustness is an extremely challenging and a major setback for trustability to A.I. The huge dimensional space (for CIFAR 10 it is 32 * 32 * 3 = 3072) makes ConvNets highly susceptible and easy to manipulate. Refer this excellent paper Florian Et Al with a major finding that adversarial examples span a contiguous subspace of large (~25) dimensionality (This finding is for MNIST which has small subspace compared to CIFAR10 of 28 * 28 * 1 = 784)
